### 1. Join the CHAOSS Slack

Slack is where our community gathers! There are a lot of channels within our workspace, BUT, we&#8217;ve reserved a channel ESPECIALLY for newcomers, and our community is highly responsive. This is [CHAOSS on Slack][1], and THIS is a channel called #newcomers where you&#8217;ll be welcomed! (Type the word **_newbie_** and see what happens!)

### 2. Attend a few CHAOSS Newcomer Hangouts

Every **Tuesday**, we have a Newcomer Hangout intended to make it easy for you to ask questions and find your place in the project with a supportive, experienced CHAOSS maintainer.

You can join the hours from **9am &#8211; 10am (USA Central Time)** through our Zoom channel: [https://zoom.us/my/chaoss.][2]

If you are in the Asia-Pacific region, you can join the Newcomer Hangout that happens at the beginning of the CHAOSS Asia Community Meeting, at **8:00 am (IST), every other Thursday**.

### 3. Attend some weekly CHAOSS Community Meetings

Every Tuesday, the community comes together to discuss anything that pertains to the whole community. This is also the place where we share announcements and news. You can participate or just listen. It&#8217;s really up to you!

You can join this call from 11am &#8211; 12pm (USA Central Time) through our Zoom channel: <https://zoom.us/my/chaoss>.

We also have regional Community Calls, which you are welcome to join:

  * CHAOSS Africa: 3:00 pm (WAT) every other Thursday
  * CHAOSS Asia: 8:00 am (IST) every other Thursday

Check the [CHAOSS calendar][3] for details!

### 4. Watch some recordings of previous meetings

We record and post virtually all of our CHAOSS meetings, and going back to watch some of the previous meetings from different working groups is a great way to begin to understand all the moving parts of CHAOSS. To see a list of playlists/categories of meetings, go to our [CHAOSStube YouTube channel][4]. (You can also listen to our CHAOSScast podcast right from there!)

### 5. Visit our CHAOSS Education Portal

We are creating short 3-5 minute videos on everything CHAOSS and everything open source for those new to the community. These are highly recommended for folks new to CHAOSS or open source. You can learn more about this on the [Education Page][5].

### (Optional) Request a Tour Guide

If you find you still aren&#8217;t sure where to go or how to contribute to CHAOSS, you can ask for a Tour Guide in the #newcomers Slack channel, and you&#8217;ll be connected with an experienced CHAOTIC who can help you find your way in a 1:1 setting.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[/vc\_column\][/vc\_row]
