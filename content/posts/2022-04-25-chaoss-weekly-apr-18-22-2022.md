### <span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSS is Participating in Google Season of Docs!</span>

We are very happy to announce that we have been selected to participate in this year&#8217;s [Google Season of Docs][1] mentorship program! If you are a technical writer and are interested in participating, we recommend you read the following:

  * [GSoD guideline][2]s
  * [CHAOSS proposal with potential projects][3]
  * [CHAOSS Instructions for potential applicants][4]

Best of luck to all applicants!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">New CHAOSScast Released: Episode 57 &#8211; The Ins and Outs of Large Scale Research with Hilary Carter</span>

This week we were so honored to be joined by Hilary Carter, the VP Research for the Linux Foundation! Hilary chats with host Georg Link, and panelists Kate Stewart and Sophia Vargas about the way that the LF approaches the large scale research projects that many others rely on in their work. They talk about the planning process, creating surveys and collecting trace data, and how we can all collaborate and share insights with each other. This is an episode you won&#8217;t want to miss, and you can listen here: <https://podcast.chaoss.community/57>.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">New Issue Templates in the Working Groups&#8217; Repos</span>

Shout out to Matt Germonprez who set up all the working group repositories with [three issue templates:][5]

  * [Template for New Metric Ideas][6]
  * [Template for Metric Release Candidates][7]
  * [Template for Revising Old Metrics][8]

These templates include guidance for form and structure and when applicable, checklists for metrics quality assurances. Standardizing them across working groups is a great step toward making it easier for contributors to open issues, ensuring we are collecting all the necessary information, and completing all the steps for releasing our metrics.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">Reminder: </span><span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSScon EU 2022 CfP is Open</span>

[CHAOSScon EU 2022][9] will be happening on September 12, in Dublin Ireland, in conjunction with [Open Source Summit Europe][10], and we are very pleased to announce that our CfP is open! We are interested in a variety of topics and presentation styles, so check out the [CfP details][9] on the CHAOSS website. **The deadline to apply is May 30**, so you have some time to draft that talk. We want to hear from you! (Sponsorship opportunities are also available if you&#8217;d like more information about those too).\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">Reminder: DEI Event Badging Reviewer Appreciation Event June 16</span>

Please join us on **June 16, 2022 at 8:00 am US Central/Chicago time** ([convert to your time][11]) as we celebrate our amazing team of reviewers and the success of the DEI Badging Initiative! This will be a fun, informal virtual event, and of course, there is no cost to participate. You can [register for the event here][12].\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
