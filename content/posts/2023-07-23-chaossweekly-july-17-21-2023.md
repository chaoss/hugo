### Thank you, FOSSY! {.has-vivid-red-color.has-text-color.wp-block-heading}

FOSSY was a wonderful conference and the first time we had an official CHAOSS booth presence. We introduced lots of folks to CHAOSS and caught up with a lot of old friends as well. We came back with some ideas about how we can improve the booth for the next conference and some excellent insights as to how folks are using and thinking about open source community metrics. Thank you to everyone who stopped by to say hello!<figure class="wp-block-image size-large">

### Context Working Group Liaisons Needed {.has-vivid-red-color.has-text-color.wp-block-heading}

We are currently looking for help with a Context Working Group Liaison Role. In our #general Slack channel, Matt Germonprez [explains what we&#8217;re looking for exactly][1]:

<div class="wp-block-group">
  <div class="wp-block-group__inner-container is-layout-flow wp-block-group-is-layout-flow">
    <div class="wp-block-group">
      <div class="wp-block-group__inner-container is-layout-flow wp-block-group-is-layout-flow">
        <div class="wp-block-group">
          <div class="wp-block-group__inner-container is-layout-flow wp-block-group-is-layout-flow">
            <blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
              <p>
                As part of CHAOSS we currently have three different groups considering metrics and metrics models in their own particular context. The current groups include:
              </p>
            </blockquote>
            
            <blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
              <p>
                1. Corporate Open Source Program Offices
              </p>
              
              <p>
                2. University Open Source Efforts
              </p>
              
              <p>
                3. Scientific Software Efforts
              </p>
            </blockquote>
          </div>
        </div>
        
        <p>
          Matt goes on to say:
        </p>
        
        <div class="wp-block-group">
          <div class="wp-block-group__inner-container is-layout-flow wp-block-group-is-layout-flow">
            <blockquote class="wp-block-quote is-layout-flow wp-block-quote-is-layout-flow">
              <p>
                In each of these groups, we ask participants to speak about the different metrics and metrics models that could support work in their particular context. As part of this, we have the actual creation of metrics and metrics models done in the CHAOSS Common WG &#8212; so members of the three different groups don&#8217;t have to spend too much time in the details associated with metrics/model creation.As such, we could use a few community members as liaisons between the different groups listed above and the CHAOSS Common WG. What would this entail?
              </p>
            </blockquote>
            
            <ol class="wp-block-list">
              <li>
                1-2 liaisons for each of the groups listed above (total of six liaisons)
              </li>
              <li>
                You would attend meeting for the group you are a liaison for (i.e., Scientific Software) as well as the CHAOSS Common WG meeting. This would be about 4 meetings per month.
              </li>
              <li>
                You would listen to the conversation that is happening in your respective context group (i.e., Scientific Software) and identify/document when the group has identified new metrics/models that need to be developed.
              </li>
              <li>
                You would bring the metrics/models ideas to the CHAOSS Common WG and talk about them in that meeting.
              </li>
              <li>
                Once it is decided that a new metric/model needs to be developed, you would create a preliminary draft of the metric/model, using existing templates.
              </li>
              <li>
                The metric/model would then be iterated on between your group (i.e., Scientific Software) and the Common WG &#8212; collaboratively developing the metric/model.
              </li>
            </ol>
          </div>
        </div>
        
        <p>
          If you have questions, please ask them in the #general channel, or reach out to Matt G or Elizabeth Barron. 
        </p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
