### <span style="font-family: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, Oxygen-Sans, Ubuntu, Cantarell, 'Helvetica Neue', sans-serif; color: #008080;">CHAOSScast Episode 86: Chat with the Turing Institute: Using AI Ethically with the Power of Open Source </span>

The lastest episode of CHAOSScast features hosts Alice Sowerby and Dawn Foster and guests Aida Mehonic, Kirstie Whittaker, and Malvika Sharan from the Alan Turing Institute. The discussion was centered around data science and AI and how open source in particular can have an impact on the ethics, accessibility and impact of artificial intelligence. The group also discusses community building, AI ethics, research, and projects like The Turing Way. This is a fantastic episode that you won&#8217;t want to miss, especially if you have an interest in AI and the future implications. ### <span style="color: #008080;">CHAOTIC of the Week: Peculiar Umeh!</span>

Please join us in celebrating our CHAOTIC of the Week: Peculiar Umeh!

<p class="p1">
  <strong><i>A little about Peculiar:</i></strong>
</p>

<span style="font-weight: 400;"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-medium wp-image-5764 alignleft" src="{{ baseURL }}wp-content/uploads/2024/06/IMG-20201020-WA0015-287x300.jpg" alt="" width="287" height="300" srcset="{{ baseURL }}wp-content/uploads/2024/06/IMG-20201020-WA0015-287x300.jpg 287w, {{ baseURL }}wp-content/uploads/2024/06/IMG-20201020-WA0015-200x209.jpg 200w, {{ baseURL }}wp-content/uploads/2024/06/IMG-20201020-WA0015.jpg 667w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 287px) 100vw, 287px" />Peculiar is an open-source advocate, mobile developer, and project manager. With a strong passion for project/program management, I excel in developer education, training, and mentoring new contributors. A Google Certified Project Manager and Certified Scrum Master.  I continually hone my skills when not engaged in open-source initiatives, build projects and mentor others. I enjoy playing volleyball and football with a local team during my free time. Peculiar is open to opportunities and is available for roles as a Project Manager, Software/Agile Project Manager, or Scrum Master.</span>

<p class="p1">
  <strong><i>What Peculiar works on at CHAOSS:</i></strong>
</p>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">At CHAOSS, I serve as the Project Management Co-chair and the Project Manager for the CHAOSS Education Project. Additionally, I am a DEI event badger and a member of the DEI and Metrics development working groups. In these groups, we collaborate to discuss and develop CHAOSS Metrics through individual contributions. I have contributed to CHAOSS Slackbot and Accessibility projects alongside others. I am also part of the organising team for CHAOSS Africa Disability and Inclusion.</span>

<p class="p1">
  <strong><i>Peculiar&#8217;s advice to newcomers to open source:</i></strong>
</p>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">CHAOSS community is a fantastic environment for learning about open source and personal growth. CHAOTICS are ready to guide you and provide opportunities for leadership and showcasing your skills. If you are new to CHAOSS, take your time to understand how the community operates and don&#8217;t hesitate to ask questions about anything unclear. Most importantly, join CHAOSS onboarding meetings with Elizabeth to get you started and join CHAOSS meetings and listen carefully during discussions. Then, find a project to contribute to, we have maintainers and leads ready to assist you. </span>

<p class="p1">
  <strong><i>Special thanks from Elizabeth and the CHAOSS Community:</i></strong>
</p>

<p class="p1">
  Peculiar joined CHAOSS a little over a year ago and the impact she has made has been remarkable! She is already a leader in the community and a consistent attendee at several of our working group meetings. Peculiar always makes time to greet newcomers to the community and cares deeply about making everyone feel welcome. She has been a constant driver of progress within the CHAOSS community, especially with several of our internal projects. We are incredibly grateful for the many hours Peculiar has spent with CHAOSS and the work she has done here! And on a personal note, it has been lovely to get to know Peculiar and I&#8217;m privileged to call her my friend. We are so glad you&#8217;re here, Peculiar, and thank you for everything!
