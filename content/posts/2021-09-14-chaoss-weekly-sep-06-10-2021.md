### <span style="color: #993300;">Join the GrimoireLab Hackathon and Bring Your Ideas to Life!</span>

It&#8217;s no secret that the GrimoireLab toolset can be extremely helpful for researchers and data scientists. As such, as part of the [2022 Mining Software Repositories Conference (MSR 2022][1]), the GrimoireLab team will be hosting the (virtual) [Hackathon Track][2].

[From the MSR 2022 site][2]:

<p style="padding-left: 40px;">
  <em>The GrimoireLab MSR Virtual Hackathon will provide activities typical of the in-person hackathon virtually. For example, defining research questions, forming teams, and scoping problems. Organizers will provide advice on the best ways to conduct data processing and improve performance. The hackathon will also provide the opportunity for participants to work with world-class researchers on relevant problems and research questions.</em>
</p>

<p style="padding-left: 40px;">
  <em>Please join if you are concerned about better understanding software development and building tools to help researchers and stakeholders better understand how specific software projects perform. Also, if you are interested in adding another tool to your toolbox, the hackathon may help you in your future research. In this hackathon, we will focus on the analysis of free, open source software (FOSS) projects providing free access to their development data, but results will be of potential interest to the industry at large, and of course, to the research community.</em>
</p>

The Hackathon and Conference are happening May 23-24, 2022, but you need to show your intent to participate, and submit any project ideas by **October 18, 2021**. Details on how to participate can be found in the [MSR 2022 Hackathon GitHub repository.][3]\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">You Have 2 More Weeks to Help Review Metrics</span>

As you probably already know, the full month of September is dedicated to our public comment period and review of the proposed metrics for our **2021-10** release. That means you have a little over 2 weeks left to add your input.

Our community is encouraged to review and add comments to these metrics candidates during this 30-day review period. **PLEASE** feel free to add your input, +1&#8217;s, or any other comments to any or all of these **16 metrics**:

  * [Programming Language Distribution][4]
  * [Collaboration Platform Activity][5]
  * [Technical Fork (re-opened)][6]
  * [Clones][7]
  * [Bot Activity][8]
  * [Event Locations][9]
  * [Psychological Safety][10]
  * [Inclusive Experience at Event][11]
  * [Event Demographics][12]
  * [Time Inclusion for Virtual Events][13]
  * [Documentation Discoverability][14]
  * [Organizational Influence][15]
  * [Contribution Attribution][16]
  * [Libyear][17]
  * [Upstream Dependency Count][18]
  * [Academic Open Source Project Impact][19]

Thank you so much for your help in making our metrics the best they can be.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
