### <span style="color: #980000;">New Newsletter Feature: Volunteer Opportunities</span>

We want to make it easier for our community members to participate in their own way, and so we are adding a new feature to the newsletter that will highlight an opportunity for you to get involved!  This week&#8217;s Volunteer Opportunity is the:

**Community Report Contributor (Cauldron)**

  * **Description of what&#8217;s needed:** Fulfill requests for a community report by generating visualizations using Cauldron (no installation needed).
  * **Skills needed:** Basic computer skills like copy/pasting and navigating a website (no coding skills required).
  * **Hours per week needed:** About 10 minutes per request. About 3-4 requests per month (currently).

If you are interested in learning more about this opportunity, reach out to Elizabeth (elizabeth@chaoss.community).\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #980000;">Two New Metrics Released from Common Working Group</span>

As part of our Continuous Release Cycle, the Common Working Group released two new metrics last week, which are now open for public review. These are:

  * <p class="gh-header-title mb-2 lh-condensed f1 mr-0 flex-auto break-word">
      <a href="https://github.com/chaoss/wg-common/issues/98"><span class="js-issue-title">Review Cycle Duration within a Change Request</span></a>
    </p>

  * [Burstiness][1]

Feel free to look at these issues and offer general feedback and comments!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #980000;">Academic Value &#8211; New Focus Area in Value Working Group</span>

During the last meeting of the Value Working Group, a new focus area was proposed that would develop metrics around the value of open source in an academic setting. We know there are a lot of people interested in this discussion and the role of OSPOs in academia, and if you would like to participate in these conversations, the Value Working Group is meeting this week (see below for details). If you just can&#8217;t wait to get in on the action, or if you&#8217;d prefer to jump in asynchronously, check out the [GitHub issue][2] that was opened.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
