### <span style="color: #980000;">No Asia-Pacific Community Meeting Next Week</span>

Because our Chinese colleagues will be on holiday next week, we decided in the last Asia-Pacific Community call to cancel next week&#8217;s meeting (May 5). The regular bi-weekly schedule will resume on May 19. See you then!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### 

### <span style="color: #980000;">CHAOSScast Episode 33: Managing Portfolios of OSS Projects with Emil Wåreus</span>

In the latest CHAOSScast episode, our panelists Georg Link, Sophia Vargas, and Armstrong Foundjem speak with Emil Wåreus from the Swedish startup debricked. They enjoyed an in-depth discussion about using analytics and computational models to understand the quality of dependencies within your open source projects, and how to mitigate bias and adverse social impacts in these complex computational models. This was a super interesting conversation that you can listen to here: [https://podcast.chaoss.community/33.][1]\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #980000;">Upcoming Workshops for Getting Started as a Contributor to CHAOSS Software</span>

In case you missed it, we have successfully launched a series of free virtual workshops to help you get started contributing to CHAOSS software (mostly centered on Augur). All workshops will be hosted on our Community Zoom (<https://zoom.us/my/chaoss>) and are from 8am &#8211; 10am CDT, 6:30pm &#8211; 8:30pm IST. You can join as an attendee, or a mentor! GSoC Students are also highly encouraged to attend.

  * April 27, 2021 &#8211; [Jupyter Notebook Visualizations][2]
  * May 24, 2021 &#8211; [Workshop: Configuring Your Computer (Getting Setup for Python Development in CHAOSS)][3]
  * June 5, 2021 &#8211; [Workshop: Restful API Development][4]
  * June 19, 2021 &#8211;  [Jupyter Notebooks: Getting Started][5]
  * July 17, 2021 &#8211; [Jupyter Notebook Visualizations][6]
  * July 31, 2021 &#8211; [Workshop: Configuring Your Computer (Getting Setup for Python Development in CHAOSS & Intro to Github Workflow)][7]
