### <span style="color: #993300;"><strong>Last Chance to Register for CHAOSScon NA 2023</strong></span> {.has-vivid-red-color.has-text-color}

<!-- /wp:heading --> CHAOSScon NA 2023, will be held in the 

**afternoon of May 9, 2023,** in conjunction with Open Source Summit North America in Vancouver BC, Canada. You can view the schedule [here][1]. <!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->In-person registration is $20 USD and registration for OSSNA is a pre-requisite. Live streaming will be available for free, but registration for OSSNA virtual is required. You can register for both events on the 

[OSSNA registration page][2]. [Sponsorship][3] is also available, so if you&#8217;re interested in sponsoring, feel free to reach out. <!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->Hope you can join us, either in-person or virtually, and don&#8217;t forget to register!

### <span style="color: #993300;"><strong>CHAOSScon Africa 2023 &#8211; Save the Date! </strong></span> {.has-vivid-red-color.has-text-color}

<!-- /wp:heading --> The CHAOSS Africa community is planning the very first CHAOSScon Africa for June 14! This will be in Lagos, Nigeria, and will be co-located with the 

[Open Source Community Africa Festival.][4] Specific details for the event are still being coordinated, so stay tuned for more information! <!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:heading {"level":3,"textColor":"vivid-red"} -->

### **<span style="color: #993300;">Recent Metrics Released</span>** In the past few months, we released a few metrics that we wanted to share with you, in case you missed them! 

  * [Change Request Closure Ratio][5] &#8211; Is the project keeping up with change requests?
  * [Release Frequency][6] &#8211; How often does a project publish a release of their software / artifact?
  * [Event Location Inclusivity][7] &#8211; Is the event located in a region where governments, cultural contexts, or society may cause harm to the physical safety and/or psychological safety of event attendees?
  * [Public Health and Safety][8] &#8211; To what extent does your event address public health through policies around masks, vaccines, COVID testing, and in infrastructure such as ventilation and air purification in indoor venues?
  * [Newcomer Experience][9] &#8211; How well does an open source community attend to welcoming newcomers? We have a variety of other metrics we are preparing to release, including: 

  * **Project Access** &#8211; To what extent does your project provide access and help those with various access needs?
  * **Communication Transparency** &#8211; <span style="font-weight: 400;">How transparent is project communication?</span>
  * **Event Location Equity** &#8211; <span style="font-weight: 400;">Where are open source project events located and are they located in regions that are accessible to all community members?</span> If you have an interest in helping build these metrics or discussing these topics, we&#8217;d love for you to join us at the applicable working group meetings, which are open to everyone. Check our 

[Community Calendar][10] for a full list of when these meetings are taking place. <!-- /wp:paragraph -->
