### <span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSScon Schedule Released  🎉</span>

We are very happy to be releasing the [CHAOSScon schedule][1] for our conference on September 30, 2021 in Seattle. Please join us either in person or via the Youtube Livestream!

Some highlights from our schedule include:

  * Keynote presentation from **Emma Irwin, Microsoft**
  * **6 talks** from industry, academia, researchers, and the CHAOSS community
  * On-site signup for **Lightning Talk** sessions

We also plan on doing an informal CHAOSS meetup while we are in Seattle, but that is still a work in progress. We hope you will join us in our [CHAOSScon Slack Channel][2] to communicate with everyone who will be at the conference or participating virtually!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSS in the News</span>

Our friends at the Rochester Institute of Technology have been using GrimoireLab (a CHAOSS software project from Bitergia) to create a portal that will help their OSPO easily display metrics for all their open source software.

Their project is called Mystic, and it&#8217;s designed to support the growing number of open source projects run by RIT staff, faculty, and students pursuing a minor in _Free and Open Source Software and Free Culture. _This field of study is new to academia, and so the RIT OSPO was really starting from square one.

You can read more about their journey with CHAOSS/GrimoireLab and Mystic at Opensource.com: _[Building an open source community health analytics platform][19], _or hear more about Mystic at [CHAOSScon][1].\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
