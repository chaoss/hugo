### <span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSS Africa Update </span>

Thanks to the efforts of [Ruth Ikegah][1] and the other core members of CHAOSS Africa, our community is growing by leaps and bounds. We now have over 85 people in the [#chaoss-africa][2] Slack channel, and we want to extend a warm welcome to all those who have recently joined. CHAOSS Africa meets bi-weekly on Thursdays at 3 pm WAT, so we encourage you to join the call. (Details on joining are below).

CHAOSS Africa will also be hosting a Twitter Spaces Thursday, August 18 at 6 PM WAT to discuss African Open Source Contributors&#8217; challenges and how we can work together to overcome them. You&#8217;ll hear from Open Source experts Samson Goddy, Bolaji Ayodeji, Ada Nduka Oyom, and Mesrenyame Dogbe. Be sure to [set a reminder to join the Twitter Spaces][3] on Thursday!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">CHAOSScast #62: Joint Podcast with Sustain</span>

In this very special episode of CHAOSScast, our panelists team up with the hosts of [Sustain&#8217;s Podcast][4] to produce a joint episode! We hope you&#8217;ll join Georg Link, Richard Littauer, Dawn Foster, Willem Jiang, Amanda Casari, and Ben Nickolls as they discuss the state of maintaining open source projects from sustainability and community health points of view. They talk about how the landscape of open source has changed, from funding to supply chain management, to community growth.

We are especially grateful for Sustain&#8217;s support and sponsorship over the past few years and it was an honor to host them on our podcast! You can listen here: [https://podcast.chaoss.community/62.][5]

\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">Volunteer Opportunity: CHAOSScon Streaming</span>

We are looking for a volunteer who will be attending CHAOSScon in person, with experience in streaming video, to assist us in setting up this service for our remote attendees. If you would like to help our A/V team with this, please reach out to Matt Germonprez or Sean Goggins on slack. We are grateful for your help!\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
