### <span style="color: #993300;">Metrics Freeze September 1, 2021, When Public Review Opens</span>

We&#8217;ve been leading up to this for a while, but the current metrics candidates will be set for public review on September 1, 2021. This means that metrics won&#8217;t be changing and no new metrics will be added for the full month of September, for our **2021-10** release.

Our community is encouraged to review and add comments to these metrics candidates during this 30-day review period. **PLEASE** feel free to add your input, +1&#8217;s, or any other comments to any or all of these **16 metrics**:

  * [Programming Language Distribution][1]
  * [Collaboration Platform Activity][2]
  * [Technical Fork (re-opened)][3]
  * [Clones][4]
  * [Bot Activity][5]
  * [Event Locations][6]
  * [Psychological Safety][7]
  * [Inclusive Experience at Event][8]
  * [Event Demographics][9]
  * [Time Inclusion for Virtual Events][10]
  * [Documentation Discoverability][11]
  * [Organizational Influence][12]
  * [Contribution Attribution][13]
  * [Libyear][14]
  * [Upstream Dependency Count][15]
  * [Academic Open Source Project Impact][16]

Thank you so much for your help in making our metrics the best they can be.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="color: #993300;">Governance Repo Renamed to Community</span>

Today in the Weekly Community Call, we decided to rename the _governance_ repo to _community_ so it can act more as a central place for asynchronous community discussions through issues. It will also help us keep track of action items that arise from the community call, make those community discussions more transparent, and give everyone in the community a place to submit an idea, question, or comment.

If something&#8217;s been on your mind, or you&#8217;d like to open an issue, we encourage you to do so! <https://github.com/chaoss/community>.\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]

### <span style="font-family: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, Oxygen-Sans, Ubuntu, Cantarell, 'Helvetica Neue', sans-serif;"><span style="color: #993300;">Metrics Quality Improvements</span></span>

Over the past few months, a team has been collaborating to develop the Metrics Checklist, which all our new metrics now abide by. This ensures that each metric goes through a process prior to being released, and it helps us keep things organized and on track. It includes steps and checks for process, content quality, and technical requirements, and you can [see the checklist here][17].

We have always upheld the highest quality standards for our metrics, but we also now include measures like translations (Chinese and Spanish), the inclusion of DEI considerations in every metric, and an opt-in list of contributors who helped developed the metric.

Having this checklist makes sure that we don&#8217;t leave anything out, so a big shout-out to the team that worked on this: Kevin Lumbard, Georg Link, Vinod Ahuja, Ritik Malik, and Yash Prakash (and others who offered their input).\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_column\_text\]
